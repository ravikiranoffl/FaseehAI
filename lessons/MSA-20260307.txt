**Today's Focus: Demonstrative Pronouns for "That" (Singular)**

Yesterday, we learned how to say "this" for masculine and feminine things. Today, we're going to expand our pointing skills and learn how to say "that" – for things that are a little further away! Just like "this," "that" will also change depending on whether the noun is masculine or feminine.

**Notes & Explanation**

In Arabic, when you want to refer to something that's not right next to you, you use demonstrative pronouns that mean "that." They work very similarly to <AUDIO: هَذَا> and <AUDIO: هَذِهِ> in terms of gender agreement:

1.  **<AUDIO: ذَلِكَ>** (dhālika): This means "that" and is used when referring to a **masculine singular** noun. Think of it as the counterpart to <AUDIO: هَذَا> for things further away.
2.  **<AUDIO: تِلْكَ>** (tilka): This means "that" and is used when referring to a **feminine singular** noun. This is the counterpart to <AUDIO: هَذِهِ> for things further away.

The rules for connecting them to nouns are exactly the same as for "this":
*   If you want to say **"That *is* a [noun]"**, the noun that follows <AUDIO: ذَلِكَ> or <AUDIO: تِلْكَ> will be indefinite (no "ال").
*   If you want to say **"That [the] [noun]"** (implying a specific, definite item), the noun that follows will be definite (with "ال").

Super easy, right? Just remember to match the gender!

**Examples in Context**
Arabic: <AUDIO: ذَلِكَ قَلَمٌ.>
Transliteration: dhālika qalamun.
Meaning: That is a pen. (Here, <AUDIO: قَلَمٌ> is masculine and indefinite.)

Arabic: <AUDIO: تِلْكَ مَكْتَبَةٌ.>
Transliteration: tilka maktabatun.
Meaning: That is a library/bookstore. (Here, <AUDIO: مَكْتَبَةٌ> is feminine and indefinite.)

Arabic: <AUDIO: ذَلِكَ الكُرْسِيُّ.>
Transliteration: dhālika al-kursiyyu.
Meaning: That chair. (Here, <AUDIO: الكُرْسِيُّ> is masculine and definite.)

Arabic: <AUDIO: تِلْكَ الطَّاوِلَةُ.>
Transliteration: tilka aṭ-ṭāwilatu.
Meaning: That table. (Here, <AUDIO: الطَّاوِلَةُ> is feminine and definite.)
